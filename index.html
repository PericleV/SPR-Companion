Welcome to SPR Companion

Your complete workflow for simulating, analyzing, and optimizing planar multilayer structures and Surface Plasmon Resonance (SPR) based sensors.

Database & Materials

Visualize and manage optical parameters (n, k, permittivity) across various wavelengths.

  • Properties: Choose from constant, dispersive, or analytical formulas (Drude, Drude-Lorentz-Kubo). Superimpose multiple materials.
  • Data Import: Import from CSV (refractiveindex.info) or digitize data directly from images. Features auto-interpolation (spline/linear) and extrapolation.
  • Material Types: Standard Bulk, Porous (define host, inclusion, Fill Factor, and EMA: Bruggeman/Maxwell Garnett), and 2D Materials (define layer thickness).
Geometry Design

Construct and visualize the simulation stack with export capabilities (PNG/SVG).

  • Configuration: Load/save configs, add/remove layers, and click to apply Reverse Illumination.
  • Auto DBR Builder: Instantly generate Distributed Bragg Reflectors by defining materials, thicknesses, periods, and defects.
  • Smart Detection: Automatically detects material types, seamlessly handling 2D materials and layer counts.
Setup & Simulation

Establish parameters for Angular or Spectral interrogation.

  • Outputs & Metrics: Calculate R, T, A, phiR, phiT. Track FWHM, Min Res, Bandgap, and Sensitivity (customize delta n and select specific active layers).
  • Advanced Analysis: Define Regions of Interest (ROI) for multiple peaks. Fit curves using Lorentz, Fano, or Coupled Oscillators.
  • Field Distribution: Visualize field intensity through the sensor depth. Export all results to CSV.
Parameter Sweep

Analyze responses by sweeping single or multiple parameters (1D/2D).

  • Sweep Types: Perform Fast Single Point scans, or comprehensive Angle/Wavelength Scans to extract performance metrics.
  • Visualization: Generate 2D heatmaps, dispersion curves, and calculate performance matrices across parameter combinations.
Optimization

Optimize the structure using SOGA, NSGA-II, PSO, or Gradient Descent.

  • Variables: Optimize thickness, material choices (scrambler), DBR periods, 2D layer count, and Porous FF.
  • Objectives: Target global/local reflectivity, FWHM, Sensitivity, or use curve fitting. Build composite objectives with constraints.
  • Integration: Select Pareto front solutions (NSGA-II) and auto-load optimized geometries directly into the designer.
